# Five Stages of Ethical Hacking

Ethical hacking follows a structured process used to understand how attackers approach systems so security weaknesses can be identified
and fixed responsibly.

---

## 1. Reconnaissance
Reconnaissance is the initial stage where information about the target
is collected. This includes understanding the targetâ€™s environment,
publicly available details, and potential attack surfaces.

This stage can be passive (no direct interaction) or active
(direct interaction with authorization).

---

## 2. Scanning
Scanning involves identifying live systems, open ports, services,
and possible vulnerabilities within the target environment.

It helps determine how systems are exposed and which areas require
further security analysis.

---

## 3. Gaining Access
In this stage, identified vulnerabilities are used to gain unauthorized access to a system.

Ethical hackers perform controlled actions to prove the existence
of weaknesses without causing damage.

---

## 4. Maintaining Access
Maintaining access focuses on understanding how attackers could
retain control over a compromised system for an extended period.

This stage highlights risks such as privilege escalation and
persistence mechanisms.

---

## 5. Clearing Tracks
Clearing tracks involves studying techniques used by attackers to
hide their activities and avoid detection.

Learning this stage helps security teams improve logging,
monitoring, and incident detection capabilities.
